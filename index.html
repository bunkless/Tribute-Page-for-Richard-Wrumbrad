Heroes of the Faith: Richard Wurmbrand

A Heart That Chose Love Over Fear

Richard Wurmbrand: A Timeline of Life

1909
Birth in Iași. Richard Wurmbrand was born on March 24th in Iași, Romania, into a Jewish family.
1938
Conversion to Christianity. Richard and his wife, Sabina, converted to Christianity, an event that completely redefined their lives.
1948
First Arrest (9 years). He was arrested by the Securitate for the first time and sentenced to nine years in prison for "activity against the social order."
1964
Final Release and Ransom. He was released through ransom (a payment of $10,000 USD by a Norwegian Christian Mission) and permanently left Romania.
1966
Testimony to the U.S. Senate. He testified before the U.S. Senate Internal Security Subcommittee, showing the scars of his torture. His testimony captured worldwide attention.
1967
Founds VOM and Publishes "Tortured for Christ." He founded "Voice of the Martyrs" and published his most famous book.
2001
Death. He passed away on February 17th in Glendale, California, at the age of 91.
